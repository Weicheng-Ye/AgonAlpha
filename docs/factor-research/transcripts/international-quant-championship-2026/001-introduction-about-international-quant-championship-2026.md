## Transcript

We will be discussing the IQC, or the International Quant Championship 2026.

The IQC is a global, multi-stage competition that focuses on quantitative finance research and modeling. This team-based event provides a platform for participants to showcase their skills. One of the most exciting aspects of the IQC is that participants have the opportunity to apply their quantitative skills at the intersection of data and financial markets. This is achieved by developing and backtesting alphas on the WorldQuant Brain simulation platform, where the competition takes place.

For those unfamiliar with the term, WorldQuant defines alphas as mathematical models that aim to predict future price movements of various financial instruments. This means that participants will be tasked with creating mathematical models that can forecast how the prices of different financial instruments may change over time. This is a crucial aspect of financial market analysis, and the competition provides a practical platform for participants to hone and showcase their skills in this area.

**Impressive Statistics from IQC 2024**

Let’s look at some impressive statistics from last year’s International Quant Championship 2024, whose global finals were held in Singapore:

- Over 37,000 individuals registered for the competition, showcasing the widespread interest in quantitative finance modeling across the globe.
- Students from over 5,300 universities were represented.
- Participants came from over 111 countries, reflecting the global relevance and appeal of quantitative finance modeling.
- Out of the 37,000+ participants, 13 teams (26 people) made it to the finals in Singapore.
- After the competition, 12 employment opportunities (internships or full-time roles) were offered to Stage 3 finalists in IQC 2024.

**Competition Format**

Now, let’s delve into the format of this year’s International Quant Championship.

- **Registration:** Registration for the competition is entirely free, ensuring accessibility for all interested participants, without any financial barriers. WorldQuant’s ethos is: “Talent is global, opportunity is not.” We aim to make quant finance accessible to all.
- **Team Composition:** A team can have 1 to 4 members. This flexible team size allows for a range of participation, from individuals who prefer to work alone to those who thrive in a team-based environment. However, all team members must be from the same university. Teams can include students, alumni, or faculty members from any major or degree program.

Once teams are formed and registered, they move on to the competition stage, where participants earn scores by submitting their models on WorldQuant Brain, the simulation platform.

**Key Dates and Stages**

- **Registration Period:** Starts on March 18 and concludes on May 14. During this period, IQC participants can create new teams, join existing teams, or switch among teams. At any given time, you can only be part of one team. After May 14, 2024, teams will be locked for the duration of the IQC, and no further changes to team structures will be allowed.
- **Stage 1: Qualifier Round:** Begins March 18 and ends May 19. Teams compete by submitting models on Brain and earning scores based on their performance.
- **Stage 2: National or Regional Round:** Starts May 27 and continues until mid-July. The first round of this stage is a virtual qualifying round where teams continue submitting alphas and accumulate points. The top teams on the leaderboard move on to the Stage 2 finals (national or regional finals), where they present and explain their models.
- **Stage 3: Global Finals:** Winners from the national or regional finals are invited to participate in the Global Finals, which take place from late July to mid-September in Singapore. A unique aspect of this stage is the surprise problem statement that teams must solve and present at the global finals.
